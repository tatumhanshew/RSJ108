How to Make an Engaging Twitter Post With a Graphic

Assembly time: 30 minutes

Having trouble with Twitter engagement? Follow this tutorial on how to create a graphic for a Twitter post!

  • Things You Should Know

  • Use Adobe Illustrator or Photoshop to create graphics
  • Use related hashtags to reach a wider audience
  • Tag relevant people
  • Use Adobe Color to find colors for your graphics that are readable
  • Use vectors in your designs that relate to your message

  1. Open a new Adobe Illustrator file, change the width to 1600 pixels and the height to 900 pixels, then click create.

  2. Open Adobe Color, click the 'create' tab.

    This will help you choose colors for your graphic that contrast well with each other.

  3. Click 'accessibility tools' and experiment with different color combinations. Pick two colors with a contrast ration of 3 or higher.

  4. Copy the hex codes of your chosen colors into your notes app or a Google Doc.

  5. Open your Illustrator file, choose the 'rectangle' tool on the sidebar, then spread it to the size of your canvas.

    You can also make the rectangle a gradient by choosing the graident tool.

  6. Click 'fill' on the right of the canvas, then click the pallette icon.

  7. Paste one of your hex codes into the box.

  8. Search for a quote or phrase related to your Tweet on Google.

  9. Find a font(s) on DaFont and download.

  10. Select the 'text' tool on Illustrator.

  11. Click the font options below Character on the right hand side and find your chosen font.

  12. Resize the letters to your chosen size, usually greater than 72.

  13. Type your quote or phrase into the text box.

    Make sure to credit the person who created the quote or phrase!

  14. Paste your other chosen hex code into the text color option.

  15. Center your quote on the canvas.

    This creates a symmetrical look that appeals to the viewers' eyes.

  16. Find transparent vectors or graphics that relate to your quote, then download.

    If the images are not transparent, use removebg.com to remove the background.

  17. Drag and drop your image(s) into Illustrator.

  18. Resize the image(s) and place accordingly.

  19. Once finished, click 'file,' then 'export,' then 'save for web.'

  20. Open Twitter and click 'Tweet.'

  21. Drag and drop your graphic into the box.

  22. Create a caption that relates to your quote or phrase.

    Use hashtags and tag relevant people!

  23. Click 'Tweet'
